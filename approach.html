Methodology

A Systematic, Multi-Scale Approach

Exploration at PMH progresses systematically from regional analysis to drill-ready targets. Each phase narrows the search area while increasing geological confidence.

1
Regional Scale
Tectonic Framework & Target Generation
Map crustal architecture and tectonic evolution to define metallogenic belts and identify first-order targets across Africa, South America, and Central Asia.
2
Project Scale
Multi-Layer Data Integration
Integrate geological mapping, geochemical sampling, geophysical surveys, and satellite imagery into a unified targeting model.
3
Target Scale
Validation & Drill Planning
Field verification, detailed sampling, and drill planning — the definitive test of any exploration hypothesis, overseen across multiple continents.

Satellite Data Sources

The multi-source satellite platforms powering our remote sensing analysis. Click any card to explore technical specifications and exploration applications.

Landsat-8
NASA / USGS
Workhorse multi-spectral platform with 11 bands spanning VIS to TIR. Foundation dataset for regional alteration mapping.
Multi-Spectral 30m GSD Free
▶ Specifications
Bands11 (OLI + TIRS)
Spatial Resolution30m (MS) / 15m (PAN)
Revisit Period16 days
Swath Width185 km
Spectral Range0.43 – 12.51 μm
PMH Application
Regional-scale iron oxide and clay alteration mapping using band ratios (6/7, 4/2). Time-series analysis for vegetation anomaly detection over concealed mineralisation.
ASTER
NASA / METI (Japan)
14-band instrument with unique TIR capability for silicate mineral discrimination and thermal anomaly detection.
Hyperspectral 15–90m GSD TIR
▶ Specifications
Bands14 (VNIR + SWIR + TIR)
Spatial Resolution15m (VNIR) / 30m (SWIR) / 90m (TIR)
Revisit Period16 days
Swath Width60 km
Spectral Range0.52 – 11.65 μm
PMH Application
SWIR band ratios for precise clay mineral identification (kaolinite vs illite vs alunite). TIR emissivity for quartz-rich silicification and carbonate discrimination in target zones.
Sentinel-1
ESA / Copernicus
C-band SAR constellation providing all-weather, day-night imaging for structural analysis and ground deformation monitoring.
SAR / Radar 5–20m Free
▶ Specifications
FrequencyC-band (5.405 GHz)
Spatial Resolution5 × 20m (IW mode)
Revisit Period6 days (constellation)
Swath Width250 km
PolarisationVV + VH (dual-pol)
PMH Application
Structural lineament extraction from SAR backscatter. InSAR coherence analysis for identifying active fault zones and deep-seated structures controlling mineralisation corridors.
Sentinel-2
ESA / Copernicus
13-band high-revisit multi-spectral imager optimised for land monitoring with dedicated red-edge bands for vegetation stress analysis.
Multi-Spectral 10–60m GSD Free
▶ Specifications
Bands13 (MSI)
Spatial Resolution10m (VIS) / 20m (NIR/SWIR) / 60m (atm)
Revisit Period5 days (constellation)
Swath Width290 km
Spectral Range0.44 – 2.19 μm
PMH Application
High-frequency monitoring of exploration tenements. Red-edge bands detect vegetation stress anomalies over shallow mineralisation. SWIR ratios complement Landsat for alteration mapping.
WorldView-2
Maxar Technologies
Very high-resolution 8-band commercial satellite enabling sub-metre structural mapping and detailed alteration zone delineation.
VHR 0.46m PAN Commercial
▶ Specifications
Bands8 (MS) + 1 (PAN)
Spatial Resolution0.46m (PAN) / 1.84m (MS)
Revisit Period1.1 days (off-nadir)
Swath Width16.4 km
Unique BandsCoastal, Yellow, Red Edge, NIR2
PMH Application
Targeted high-resolution analysis of priority drill areas. Coastal and yellow bands enable iron oxide sub-type discrimination. Stereo-pair DEM generation for detailed structural mapping.
JL-1 (Jilin-1)
Chang Guang Satellite Technology
Rapidly expanding Chinese commercial constellation offering high-resolution optical and video imaging with fast revisit capability.
VHR Optical 0.72m Video
▶ Specifications
Constellation138+ satellites (growing)
Spatial Resolution0.72m (PAN) / 2.88m (MS)
Revisit Period<1 day (constellation)

Rapid-revisit monitoring of exploration sites and mining operations. Video capability for real-time site assessment. Cost-effective alternative to Western VHR platforms for routine monitoring.
How We Partner

Building Local Partnerships. Investing in Early-Stage Assets.

We believe the best projects are built together. PMH actively seeks partnerships with local license holders, community leaders, junior explorers, and independent operators across our target regions. We are open to all forms of collaboration, from joint ventures and earn-in agreements to full project acquisitions, and we welcome proposals at any stage.

If you have geological potential and local knowledge, we bring the capital, technical team, and international infrastructure to move a project forward.

We Are Actively Looking for Early-Stage Investment Opportunities In:
Gold
Core Focus

Our primary commodity and the foundation of our portfolio. We are seeking gold exploration and near-production assets across West and Southern Africa, from grassroots targets to advanced projects with defined resources.

Copper
Strategic Priority

A critical metal for the global energy transition. We are interested in copper exploration opportunities in proven geological belts, particularly where polymetallic potential adds upside.

Rare Earths & Critical Metals
Selective Interest

We evaluate high-quality rare earth and critical metal opportunities on a selective basis, focusing on jurisdictions with clear regulatory pathways and geological merit.

What We Bring to a Partnership
→Exploration and development capital
→In-house geological, geochemical, and geophysical expertise
→Satellite remote sensing and AI-driven target generation
→International compliance, trading, and offtake infrastructure
→Long-term commitment, we don’t walk away after one season
What We Look For in a Partner
→Genuine local knowledge and community trust
→Licenses in geologically prospective areas
→Transparency and willingness to operate within structured frameworks
→Alignment on long-term value creation, not short-term extraction
→Openness to working together, we are flexible on deal structure

Speed Without Compromise

As a privately capitalised firm, we are not constrained by fundraising cycles or public reporting timelines. When the data supports a decision, we act, at a pace listed companies cannot match, with the rigour institutional partners expect.

Our Commitment to Responsible Operations

We recognise that mineral exploration operates within communities, ecosystems, and sovereign territories that deserve respect. PMH is committed to responsible environmental stewardship, transparent engagement with local stakeholders, and contributing meaningfully to the social and economic development of the regions where we work. In every jurisdiction, we seek to build partnerships that create lasting value, not just for our investors, but for the people and landscapes that make our work possible.
